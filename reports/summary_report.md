# Student Performance Data Analysis – Summary Report

## Objective

* Explore and analyze the Student Performance dataset.
* Perform basic data cleaning and inspection.
* Visualize score distributions.
* Generate actionable insights for educators.

## Dataset Overview

* Total Records: 1000
* Total Features: 8
* Score Columns:

  * Math Score
  * Reading Score
  * Writing Score

## Data Cleaning Performed

* Checked for missing values.

  * Result: No missing values found.
* Checked for duplicate records.

  * Result: No duplicate records found.
* Standardized column names:

  * Converted to lowercase
  * Replaced spaces with underscores
  * Replaced "/" with "_"

## Statistical Summary

| Subject | Average Score |
| ------- | ------------- |
| Math    | 66.09         |
| Reading | 69.17         |
| Writing | 68.05         |

### Observation

* Reading has the highest average score.
* Mathematics has the lowest average score.

## Correlation Analysis

* Reading ↔ Writing: 0.95
* Math ↔ Reading: 0.82
* Math ↔ Writing: 0.80

### Observation

* Reading and writing scores are strongly related.
* Students who perform well in reading generally perform well in writing.

## Impact of Test Preparation Course

### Students Who Completed Preparation

* Math: 69.70
* Reading: 73.89
* Writing: 74.42

### Students Who Did Not Complete Preparation

* Math: 64.08
* Reading: 66.53
* Writing: 64.50

### Observation

* Students who completed the preparation course scored significantly higher across all subjects.

## Key Insights

1. Test preparation positively impacts student performance.
2. Reading and writing skills are strongly connected.
3. Most students score within the moderate performance range.
4. Mathematics may require additional academic support.

## Recommendations

* Encourage students to participate in test preparation programs.
* Provide extra support for mathematics.
* Strengthen reading development initiatives.
* Offer targeted help to below-average performers.

## Conclusion

The dataset was successfully cleaned, analyzed, and visualized. The analysis shows that test preparation programs improve performance and that strong reading skills are closely associated with better writing performance.
